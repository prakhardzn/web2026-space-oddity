Hopes and Fears for Art William Morris · 1882

The Art of the People

That thing which I understand by real art is the expression by man of his pleasure in labour. I do not believe he can be happy in his labour without expressing that happiness.

That is the only real art there is, the only art which will be an instrument to the progress of the world, and not a hindrance.

The Lesser Arts

Nothing can be a work of art which is not useful; that is to say, which does not minister to the body when well under command of the mind, or which does not amuse, soothe, or elevate the mind in a healthy state.

A Happiness to the Maker

An art made by the people, and for the people, as a happiness to the maker and the user.
